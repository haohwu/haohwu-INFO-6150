About Beijing

Beijing, the capital of China, boasts a rich history spanning over 3,000 years and is known for its cultural significance as the imperial seat of various dynasties. Home to iconic landmarks like the Forbidden City and Tiananmen Square, the city seamlessly blends ancient traditions with modernity. Renowned for its diverse cuisine, including the world-famous Peking duck, Beijing offers a culinary adventure amidst its bustling streets and traditional hutongs. As a global metropolis, Beijing hosted the 2008 Summer Olympics, showcasing its architectural prowess and international prominence.
